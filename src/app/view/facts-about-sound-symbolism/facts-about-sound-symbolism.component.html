The Sound Of Success: How Brands Can Benefit From Phonetics

Research shows that brands labelled with proper phonetics are better perceived by 65%.

Note: This article is based on findings from multiple research studies. It doesn't include any opinionated thought.

The Boring Dictionary Stuff
Phonetics is the study and classification of speech sounds. Sound Symbolism is the term used for the categories of speech sounds and these categories can be continuums such as big/small, fast/slow, light/heavy, roung/angular, etc.


The Prologue
Sounds convey certain types of meaning. Several studies have demonstrated that the fit between a product category and brand name can affect recall, preference, and inference of the brand.


Caution
Studies show that some sounds, such as the sound [u], in the word puke, are connoted with disgust and dislike.
Brands should avoid these sounds altogether.

For those that don't avoid such sounds, they can assume their fate with the findings that in US Presidential Elections Outcomes of 1824-1992, and some other election data, candidates with unfavorable names lost 73-83% of the elections.


The Crux
Research shows that 65% of people prefer brands to have proper phonetics. The same insight can also be inferred as brands labeled with proper phonetics are 65% better perceived by the public.

Sound symbolism of categories is slightly more important than sound symbolism of attributes.
