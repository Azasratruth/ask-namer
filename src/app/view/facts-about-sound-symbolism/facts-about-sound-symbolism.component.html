The Sound Of Success: How Brands Can Benefit From Phonetics

Research shows that 65% of people prefer brands with names that have proper phonetics.

This article is based on findings from multiple research studies. It doesn't include any opinionated thoughts.

The Boring Dictionary Stuff
Phonetics is the study and classification of speech sounds. Sound Symbolism refers to the connotation of speech sounds. These connotations are continuums such as masculine/feminine, big/small, fast/slow, light/heavy, round/angular, etc.

Sound symbolism is a big word. So, I'll be using phonetics in its place in the article.


The Prologue
Sounds convey certain types of meaning. Studies show that brand names affect recall, preference, and perception of the brand. The way to improve all these is by using phonetics according to the product category and attributes.


Caution
The sound [u], in the word puke, connotes disgust and dislike. There are similar sounds that have negative connotations.
Brands should avoid these sounds altogether.

If brands who don't avoid these think they are in the clear, they should think again. Candidates with unfavorable names lost 83% of the time in the US Presidential elections of 1824-1992. The results are consistent in similar studies.


The Crux
Research shows that 65% of people prefer brands that have proper phonetics. These brands are also better perceived by people.
