What's in a Brand Name?

Values. Values refer to the qualities that a name has or exhibits.

Note: This article is based findings from multiple research studies and my own inferences.

Brand names can be more than just unique and meaningful. There are different values/qualities that you can have in a brand name.

Values of names are different than types of names but both concepts are mutually inclusive. Values refer to the qualities that a name has or exhibits and I'll be explaining values in this blog. You can easily find 'types of names' blogs everywhere, literally every branding site has the same one.

What makes values important is that they can set the brand perception, and if executed well, they can be good for marketing.


1) Hard Suggestive of Service / Solution
This value explains exactly what the company does.
These names are words from the English language and can be compound.
While these work great for describing the work that the company does, they are drowned out by noise on search engines.

Some examples of names with this value are American Airlines, Hotels, Home Depot.


2) Soft Suggestive of Service / Solution
This value also explains exactly what the company does but it's more subtle.
These can be a complex combination of words or words just stitched together. These names can also be composed of non-English words.

Duolingo is a great example of this.


3) Eponymous
Eponymous means that the name is derived from a personâ€™s name, mostly from the founder or a historical figure, or even fictional characters. These brands are named out of ego or laziness.

Everyone knows Tesla is named after Nikola Tesla, but it's a lesser known fun fact that Dell is named after the founder Michael Dell.
Other companies that have eponymous names are: Louis Vuitton, Disney, Adidas, Toyota, Starbucks, Burberry.

These names can provide you with value in recognition. If you have done extraordinary work and are known for it, the same can be directly associated your brand.


4) Call to Action
This value is a sub-category of Hard Suggestive of Service / Solution. It refers to having a call-to-action right in the name.
As with Hard Suggestive, while these work great for describing what the company does, they are drowned out by noise on search engines.

Examples are Get Tickets, Get Advice.


5) Speech Perception
This value allows your brand Identity to be reflected in the auditory perception of the name. This is achieved by utilizing the power of phonetics.

The effect of these names is subliminal. When people read/hear these names, they subconsciously attribute them to masculine/feminine, big/small, light/heavy, fast/slow, cheap/expensive, abundant/scarce and many more depending on the speech pattern of the name. These connotations relate to the idea, not the feeling. They are effective in 80% of the most common languages.

In the name types, this value is termed abstract names.
Famous examples are Rolex, and Kodak.


6) Increased Memorability
Some names are considerably more memorable than others. There are many models one can use to judge the name memorability, semantic, syntactic, speech perception.

Research on some of these is solid while with others, theanswers are still not clear.

I would give some examples but they vary depending on the model used to calculate memorability.


7) Connotation
This value refers to the qualitative features that names can have. These are evaluated by testing each name.
The connotation in this relates to the feeling, not the idea.

Examples are Served Today which feels welcoming.


So what's in a brand name?
All qualities that you can generate in it.
