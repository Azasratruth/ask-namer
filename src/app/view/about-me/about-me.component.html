How did a computer scientist become a namer?

Bit of a weird story.

It began when one of my professors liked my project name in university and since then he has been sending people for naming help to me. However, I only committed to it after experimenting with different fields.

According to MBTI, I’m an INFJ. Hence, I’m an intuitionist and I find human connection motivating. While MBTI is flawed, it still has its merits. A key thing to note is that intuition is convergent thinking while thought (the act of thinking) is divergent thinking.

Naming meets the complete criteria of the type of work that I would find passion in. It requires convergent thinking, it has a direct human connection that motivates me, and I find it interesting.

Since then, I’ve spent thousands of hours reading research on linguistics and naming. And with my naming expertise and tech knowledge I have been building a product for brand name diagnosis.

My Vision

To help people, who are doing something positive, get recognized by their name.
